How the Calculations Work
1
Daily Demand Rate
max(units_sold_30d ÷ 30, units_sold_90d ÷ 90)
Takes the higher of the 30-day and 90-day daily averages to avoid underestimating demand when recent sales spike.
2
Available Inventory
max(0, inventory_on_hand − committed_units)
On-hand stock minus already-committed units. Floored at zero so negative committed counts don't inflate availability.
3
Effective Inventory
available_inventory + incoming_units
Adds any confirmed inbound shipments to available stock, giving a forward-looking count rather than a static snapshot.
4
Days of Cover
effective_inventory ÷ demand_run_rate
How many days the current effective inventory will last at the current demand rate. The primary risk signal.
5
Safety Buffer
max(7, ⌈0.25 × lead_time_days⌉)
At least 7 days, or 25% of lead time (whichever is larger). Provides a cushion for shipping delays and demand variability.
6
Stockout Risk Threshold
lead_time_days + safety_buffer_days
A SKU is flagged EXPEDITE when its days of cover falls below this threshold — meaning stock will run out before a reorder arrives.
7
Reorder Quantity
max(0, (lead_time + 30) × demand − effective_inventory)
Covers the lead time plus 30 days of forward demand, minus what you already have. Rounded up to MOQ.
8
Estimated Cash for Reorder
reorder_qty × unit_cost
Only calculated when unit_cost is present in the data. A planning estimate, not an invoice.
Important Caveats & Edge Cases
  • Newly Active SKU
    A SKU with fewer than 30 days of sales history may show inflated demand rates. Cross-check against manual counts or use 90-day figures if available.
  • Off-season
    Seasonal products (e.g., holiday bundles) will show very high days-of-cover outside their season. These appear as deadstock candidates but are not necessarily distressed. Compare against your assortment calendar.
  • Verify Net of Returns
    If your on-hand count includes returned but unsellable inventory, the effective inventory will be overstated. Confirm on-hand reflects only sellable units before placing reorders.
Bundles and kits are not exploded into components. This is a planning aid, not a forecast guarantee.
